3:T661,<p>The Inflation Reduction Act and Bipartisan Infrastructure Law contributed much-needed progress but have not completed the transition — there is still enormous work ahead to decarbonize our economy.</p>
<p>I recently joined the Earth911 podcast to discuss the current state of the energy transition, what the IRA has accomplished, and what challenges remain.</p>
<h2>The State of the Transition</h2>
<p>The clean energy transition is accelerating, but not fast enough. While solar and storage deployment has hit record levels, we're still deploying at a fraction of the pace needed to meet our climate targets. The good news: the economics have never been better, the technology has never been more proven, and the policy environment — despite its uncertainties — is more supportive than ever.</p>
<h2>What's Working</h2>
<ul>
<li>Solar costs continue to decline</li>
<li>Battery storage is becoming economically competitive</li>
<li>Corporate demand for clean energy is at all-time highs</li>
<li>The workforce is growing (though not fast enough)</li>
</ul>
<h2>What Needs to Change</h2>
<ul>
<li>Permitting and interconnection timelines remain a bottleneck</li>
<li>Policy uncertainty creates investment hesitation</li>
<li>The workforce gap is real and growing</li>
<li>Grid infrastructure needs massive investment</li>
</ul>
<p>The energy transition is not a question of if — it's a question of how fast. Every month we delay costs us in both economic and environmental terms.</p>
