The Inflation Reduction Act and Bipartisan Infrastructure Law contributed much-needed progress but have not completed the transition — there is still enormous work ahead to decarbonize our economy.

I recently joined the Earth911 podcast to discuss the current state of the energy transition, what the IRA has accomplished, and what challenges remain.

## The State of the Transition

The clean energy transition is accelerating, but not fast enough. While solar and storage deployment has hit record levels, we're still deploying at a fraction of the pace needed to meet our climate targets. The good news: the economics have never been better, the technology has never been more proven, and the policy environment — despite its uncertainties — is more supportive than ever.

## What's Working

- Solar costs continue to decline
- Battery storage is becoming economically competitive
- Corporate demand for clean energy is at all-time highs
- The workforce is growing (though not fast enough)

## What Needs to Change

- Permitting and interconnection timelines remain a bottleneck
- Policy uncertainty creates investment hesitation
- The workforce gap is real and growing
- Grid infrastructure needs massive investment

The energy transition is not a question of if — it's a question of how fast. Every month we delay costs us in both economic and environmental terms.
