### Color Analysis & Direction for Merged Site
The logos are monochrome (black/white), giving us complete freedom on the accent palette. Neither site's current colors are particularly strong:
- The Wix site uses a generic dark navy
- The Podpage site uses a purple accent

**Recommendation:** Build a fresh, distinctive color system anchored to the black/white logo. Given the cleantech/solar context, consider:
- A deep, sophisticated green (not generic "eco green" — think forest/emerald) as the primary brand color
- A warm amber/gold accent (solar energy association)
- Rich charcoal/near-black for text and dark sections
- Clean whites and warm grays for backgrounds
- The lightning bolt element in the logo could inspire an electric accent color

## Typography Direction

### Current Typography
- **cleanpower.group:** Raleway (primary headings), Avenir LT W01 85 Heavy (some headings), Rubik Medium (UI elements), Arial/Helvetica fallbacks. Inconsistent mixing of 3+ families with no clear hierarchy.
- **cleanpowerhour.com:** Standard sans-serif from Podpage template — clean but generic, no distinctive character.

### Recommended Direction
The logo typography is bold, condensed, geometric sans-serif with strong visual weight. Font pairing should complement this:
- **Display/Headings:** A bold geometric or humanist sans-serif with character — consider Outfit, Syne, Instrument Sans, DM Sans, or Plus Jakarta Sans (all Google Fonts, none on the banned list)
- **Body:** A highly readable serif or sans-serif — consider Crimson Pro, Newsreader, or Atkinson Hyperlegible for body text
- **The pairing should feel:** Authoritative yet approachable. Modern but grounded. Clean energy, not generic corporate.
